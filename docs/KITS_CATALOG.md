# drummr: Kits Catalog

A comprehensive overview of the factory kits included with `drummr`, categorized by their design influence and sonic "feel."

---

## 1. The Heritage Collection (Hardware Emulations)
These kits aim for hardware-faithful recreations of legendary drum machines.

- **808 Reborn**: Pure bridged-T sine kicks and iconic 540/800Hz cowbells. The classic boomy soul.
- **909 Warehouse**: Gritty techno essentials. The hybrid analog/sampled character of the TR-909.
- **Linn Lite**: The controlled, produced 80s "Clap and Cabasa" flavor of the LM-1.
- **Polar Kick**: A dirtier, "RD-9" style take on the 909, with bitcrushed cymbals and FM punch.
- **Hexagon**: Sci-fi Simmons SDS-V "pew" toms and 8-bit digital crunch.

## 2. The Generative Collection (Space-Time Benders)
Kits that use the engine's advanced logic to create evolving or rhythmic textures from simple inputs.

- **Ghost Maker**: Aphex Twin/Squarepusher-inspired. Random ghost flams, micro-rolls, and hi-hat dropouts.
- **Phase Mirror**: Steve Reich's *Piano Phase* as a kit. Two kicks that slowly drift out of sync over time.
- **Polymeter Madness**: Every drum fires its own time signature (5/8, 7/8, 13/8), creating an erupting rhythmic cathedral.
- **Stutter Snare**: Footwork/RP Boo style. Dense sub-hits and swarming 1/32nd-note rolls.
- **Event Horizon**: Accelerating "bouncing ball" patterns and time-dilated snares that ring for 4 bars.

## 3. The Ambient & Texture Collection
Focused on atmosphere, organic field recordings, and sustained washes.

- **Cathedral Forever**: Eno-style drone kit. Bells that ring for 8 seconds, breathing through slow LFOs.
- **Crystal Choir**: Ethereal glass resonances and fragile vocal-like textures. Uses high-Q modal banks for tuned glass and dense granular clouds for 'choir' atmospheres.
- **Quantum Fluid**: Liquid, organic textures using heavy macro-morphing. A kit that breathes.
- **Garden at 3 AM**: Crickets, twigs, and frog throats. An unpitched, organic forest soundscape.
- **Grain Dust**: All-Granular textural clouds and scattered particles.
- **Drift**: Cinematic swells and slow-attack mallet beds.

## 4. The Industrial & Lo-Fi Collection
Abrasive, mechanical, and grit-focused kits.

- **Foundry**: Anvil hits, overdriven FM, and "Bell of Doom" cymbals. Pure metallic industry.
- **Industrial Foundry**: Grinding gears, steam pistons, and factory sirens. Machinery as percussion.
- **Magnetic Monolith**: Heavy industrial textures focused on metallic resonance and mechanical shudders. Features deep FM kicks, discordant modal bells, and bitcrushed physical modeling.
- **Lo-Fi Crate**: Crate-digging hip-hop. 12-bit grit, vinyl crackle, and aggressive bitcrush.
- **Ratchet & Wheeze**: Burial-school textures. Broken motors, asthmatic exhales, and modem squeals.

## 5. The Specialized & Experimental Collection
Niche kits exploring specific synthesis methods or ASMR-like environments.

- **Glass Forest**: Weightless, crystalline percussion built from tuned wine glasses and bowls.
- **Office After Hours**: ASMR staplers, typewriters, and pen-taps. Dry and transient-heavy.
- **Karplus Forge**: All-Physical Modeling. Resonant marimba toms and plucked-string snares.
- **Kitchen Sink Symphony**: The kitchen as a stadium. Ceramic clicks, pot booms, and water drops.
- **Neon Night**: Arcade sheen and vibrantly electronic textures.
- **Tokyo Toms**: Yamaha RX5-style inharmonic bell-toms and FM clangour.
